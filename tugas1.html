Introduction

Strawberries are sweet supple red fruits with surfaces that are seed studded. They come from a low growing plant belonging to genus Fragaria ofe the rose (Roseceae) family, enclosing with flowers lobed, foliage, and runners. They originate all through the north temprate zones.

They are the most famous and trendiest fruits in the planet (Roger). There are over 600 assortment of strawberries that vary in taste, size, and feel; all of which have similar trait; heart silhoutte, red fleshy tissue with yellow seeds shooting the surface, petite, royal, leafy green lids, and stalk that embllish their crowns (WHFoods). Detailed History

The word strawberry is coined from 'strewn' berries about the plants and thus the "srtewn berry" finally turned into "Strawberry"(Roger). They are not actual fruits of berries but distended ends of the stamen of the plant. The seeds are on external surface, rather than internal berry. There are around 200 seeds for each berry. They are low in calories and are non-fat, loaded with vitamin c, folic acid, fiber, vitamin B6 and posstasium (Roger).

Over the years they have been utilized as medications for ailments like, stained teeth, sunburns, gouts and digestion (Hopley, 9). Strawberries were served up at medieval state occasions as a show of peace, wealth, and excellence. The most well-known communal intake of strawberries is at Wimbledon annually, when strawberries and cream are eaten by very well attired Englishmen in the middle of tennis games (Hopley, 9).
